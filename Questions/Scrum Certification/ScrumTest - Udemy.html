Scrum Theory and Values - Results

Question 1: Correct
List the three pillars that uphold Scrum?

Explanation

Scrum combines four formal events for inspection and adaptation within a containing event, the Sprint. These events work because they implement the empirical Scrum pillars of transparency, inspection, and adaptation.
Question 2: Incorrect
If one or more aspects of a process deviate outside acceptable limits, when must an adjustment be made?

Explanation

If any aspects of a process deviate outside acceptable limits or if the resulting product is unacceptable, the process being applied or the materials being produced must be adjusted. The adjustment must be made as soon as possible to minimize further deviation.
Question 3: Incorrect
Scrum is not a process or technique. True or False?

Explanation

Scrum is a lightweight framework Various processes, techniques and methods can be employed within the framework. Scrum wraps around existing practices or renders them unnecessary. Scrum makes visible the relative efficacy of current management, environment, and work techniques, so that improvements can be made.
Question 4: Incorrect
Which of these are Scrum Values?

Explanation

Successful use of Scrum depends on people becoming more proficient in living five values: Commitment, Focus, Openness, Respect, and Courage
Question 5: Incorrect
What is Scrum?

Explanation

Scrum is a lightweight framework that helps people, teams and organizations generate value through adaptive solutions for complex problems.
Question 6: Correct
The Scrum guide recommends implementing the part of Scrum (events, artifacts, roles etc) that are applicable in the current project

Explanation

The Scrum framework, as outlined herein, is immutable. While implementing only parts of Scrum is possible, the result is not Scrum. Scrum exists only in its entirety and functions well as a container for other techniques, methodologies, and practices.
Question 7: Incorrect
What is Scrum is founded on? Pick two

Explanation

Scrum is founded on empiricism and lean thinking. Empiricism asserts that knowledge comes from experience and making decisions based on what is observed. Lean thinking reduces waste and focuses on the essentials.
Question 8: Incorrect
What does Cone of Uncertainty describe?

Explanation

The cone of uncertainty describes the best case uncertainty of a project as we progress through time. As more development is completed, more is known and the uncertainty and risk reduce to zero percent at the end of the project. Question 11: Incorrect
What is the fundamental unit of Scrum

Explanation

The fundamental unit of Scrum is a small team of people, a Scrum Team. The Scrum Team consists of one Scrum Master, one Product Owner, and Developers. Within a Scrum Team, there are no sub-teams or hierarchies. It is a cohesive unit of professionals focused on one objective at a time, the Product Goal.
Question 12: Correct
List two essential features a Scrum Team should possess?

Explanation

Scrum Teams are cross-functional, meaning the members have all the skills necessary to create value each Sprint. They are also self-managing, meaning they internally decide who does what, when, and how.
